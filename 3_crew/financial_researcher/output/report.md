## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Baker Hughes Company stands as a global energy technology powerhouse with a market capitalization of approximately $67.24 billion and operations spanning more than 120 countries across six continents. As the second-largest oil equipment company globally by revenue, Baker Hughes is strategically positioned at the intersection of traditional energy services and transformative clean energy technologies. The company generated $27.829 billion in revenue during 2024, representing a 9.11% year-over-year increase, with a record adjusted EBITDA of $4.83 billion achieved in 2025.

The company's strategic transformation—encapsulated in its mission to "Rewrite The Energy Equation™"—reflects a deliberate pivot toward making energy safer, cleaner, and more efficient. This positioning is evidenced by the company's aggressive expansion into high-growth segments including liquefied natural gas (LNG), data center power systems, carbon capture technologies, and AI-enabled digital solutions. The pending acquisition of Chart Industries for $13.6 billion represents a transformative move that will significantly enhance Baker Hughes' Industrial & Energy Technology (IET) segment capabilities.

Under the leadership of CEO Lorenzo Simonelli, Baker Hughes has demonstrated remarkable financial resilience and growth trajectory, with stock performance increasing 207% over the past five years and 132% since its 2017 IPO. The company's strong free cash flow generation ($8.94 billion in 2024, $2.73 billion in 2025) and record remaining performance obligations (RPO) of $35.9 billion provide substantial revenue visibility and financial flexibility for continued strategic investments.

However, Baker Hughes faces headwinds from declining North American drilling activity, geopolitical volatility, and the fundamental energy transition away from fossil fuels. Despite these challenges, the company's diversified portfolio, strong market position, and strategic focus on emerging opportunities position it favorably for the evolving global energy landscape.

### 1.3 Business Segment Structure and Performance

Baker Hughes operates through two primary business segments, each with distinct market dynamics, growth profiles, and strategic importance:

#### **Segment 1: Oilfield Services & Equipment (OFSE)**

**Purpose and Scope:**
The OFSE segment encompasses traditional oil and gas drilling, completion, and production services. This segment serves customers engaged in upstream oil and gas exploration and production, providing essential equipment and services for well drilling, completion, and production optimization.

**2024-2025 Performance:**

|--------|---------|---------|-------|
| Revenue | $3,871 million | $3,237 million | Declining |
| Sequential Change | -2% | -9% | Accelerating decline |
| Year-over-Year Change | -2% | -7% | Negative |
| Operating Income (2024) | $1,988 million | N/A | N/A |

The OFSE segment faces significant headwinds from declining North American drilling activity, regional market weakness (particularly in the Middle East), and the broader energy transition. The 9% sequential decline in Q1 2026 revenue and 7% year-over-year decline indicate accelerating weakness in this segment.

**Market Dynamics:**
- North American drilling activity has declined significantly as major oil producers moderate capital expenditures
- Middle East market weakness is impacting international operations
- Upstream spending slowdown from major integrated oil companies is reducing demand for oilfield services
- Commodity price sensitivity remains high, with customer spending correlated to crude oil prices

**Strategic Importance:**
While facing near-term headwinds, the OFSE segment remains strategically important as a cash-generating business that funds growth investments in higher-margin segments. The segment's established customer relationships, global infrastructure, and technical expertise provide a foundation for future growth as energy markets stabilize.

**Purpose and Scope:**
The IET segment encompasses gas turbines, power systems, LNG solutions, carbon capture technologies, digital platforms, and advanced industrial applications. This segment serves customers across oil and gas, power generation, industrial manufacturing, and emerging sectors such as data centers and hydrogen production.

The data center opportunity exemplifies Baker Hughes' ability to identify emerging market trends and rapidly develop competitive responses. This market segment offers attractive economics, strong growth rates, and alignment with technological trends.
